Section 01

Executive Summary

MAZAL enters the market as a quiet-luxury modest-fashion house — abayas, kaftans and scarves designed with the restraint of a maison and the warmth of home. The brand promise is captured in one word: Still. The 90-day goal is not loud discounting; it is to build a recognisable, premium, trust-rich brand that converts a warm Gulf audience into first orders and repeat clients.

The Objective

Launch MAZAL, reach the first 1,000 engaged followers per core channel, capture 2,000+ emails/WhatsApp opt-ins, and land the first 150–300 orders in 90 days.

The Strategy

Editorial organic content (IG + TikTok led), a tight influencer seeding program, retargeting-first paid ads, and a concierge retention loop via WhatsApp & email.

The Edge

A genuinely beautiful storefront, a clear point of view ("still elegance"), AED-native trust signals, and modest-luxury positioning that is under-served at this quality level.

Section 02

Brand Positioning

Positioning Statement

For the modern Gulf woman who values calm, considered elegance, MAZAL is a quiet-luxury modest-fashion house that designs abayas, kaftans and scarves made to endure — never trend-led, always timeless. Unlike fast modest-fashion and loud logo brands, MAZAL sells stillness: fluid silhouettes, warm neutral tones, and pieces worn and worn again.

Brand Pillars

  • Still / Calm — "MAZAL means Still." Restraint over noise.
  • Craft — hand-finished seams, considered to the last stitch.
  • Endurance — "designed to endure," anti-fast-fashion.
  • Warmth — Gulf hospitality, the warmth of home.
  • Modesty as power — "Modesty shaped by craft, worn with quiet confidence."

Brand Voice

  • Editorial, unhurried, warm — never salesy or shouty.
  • Short, poetic lines. Lots of white space.
  • Bilingual-ready: English with tasteful Arabic accents (الأناقة في السكون).
  • Avoid: "SALE!!!", emojis-as-noise, urgency spam.
  • Prefer: "An edit of quiet statement pieces."

Section 05

Launch Strategy — 3 Phases

Phase 1 · Days 1–30 — “The Veil Lifts” (Awareness & Trust)

Goal: establish the brand world, prove quality, and build the first audience & list. Light spend; heavy content + seeding.

  • Publish 4–5 IG/TikTok posts per week; daily Stories.
  • Seed 15–25 micro-influencers with hero pieces.
  • Launch teaser → countdown → "now open" sequence.
  • Turn on retargeting only; collect emails with STILL10.

Phase 2 · Days 31–60 — “Considered” (Traction & Conversion)

Goal: convert the warm audience; find 2–3 winning ad creatives; scale what works.

  • Introduce prospecting ads using best influencer/UGC content.
  • Run the first campaign tentpole (e.g., "The Abaya Edit").
  • Launch the Rewards / referral loop & WhatsApp broadcast list.
  • Begin weekly email; start "complete-the-look" cross-sell.

Phase 3 · Days 61–90 — “Worn & Worn Again” (Scale & Retention)

Goal: scale profitable acquisition, lift AOV & repeat rate, lock in a content engine.

  • Scale winning ad sets; add lookalike audiences.
  • Run a signature campaign with 1 macro + 5 micro influencers.
  • Push bundles (abaya + scarf + belt) & gifting.
  • Formalise UGC pipeline; first customer-photo gallery live.

Launch-Week Sequence (Days 1–7)

D-7 teaser ("Something still is coming") → D-3 monogram reveal → D-1 countdown sticker → D-Day "We're open" Reel + email + WhatsApp blast → D+1 founder story → D+2 first influencer posts go live → D+3 first UGC repost → D+5 "most-loved so far" → D+7 styling Live on Instagram.

Section 14

Paid Ads Strategy
